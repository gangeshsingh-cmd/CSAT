Day 1

Number System

CSAT mein numbers ki properties par seedha 1–2 question har saal aate hain. Yeh poori arithmetic ki neenv hai.
Concept

Classification: Natural (1,2,3…), Whole (0,1,2…), Integers (…−2,−1,0,1,2…), Rational (p/q form), Irrational (√2, π).

  • Even/Odd: Even ± Even = Even; Odd ± Odd = Even; Even ± Odd = Odd.
  • Prime: 1 se bada, sirf 1 aur khud se divisible. 2 hi ekmaatra even prime hai.
  • Place value vs Face value: Face value = digit khud; Place value = digit × uski jagah ki value.
Sum of first n naturals = n(n+1)/2
Sum of first n odd numbers = n²  ·  Sum of squares = n(n+1)(2n+1)/6

⚡ CSAT Shortcut

Kisi bhi number ke last digit se hi unit-digit nikal jaata hai. Bade product/power mein sirf last digits multiply karo — pura calculation karne ki zaroorat nahi.

Solved Example
1 se 100 tak ke sabhi natural numbers ka sum kya hoga?
(a) 4950(b) 5050(c) 5100(d) 5000
Formula: n(n+1)/2 = 100 × 101 / 2 = 5050. Direct formula lagao, ek-ek jodne ki zaroorat nahi.
Answer: (b) 5050
Practice — Solve Now

7^85 ka unit digit kya hoga?
(a) 1(b) 3(c) 7(d) 9
Q2.

(a) 12(b) 14(c) 16(d) 18
Q3.

(a) 380(b) 400(c) 420(d) 441
AnswersQ1 → c  ·  Q2 → b  ·  Q3 → b

Divisibility & Remainder

Divisibility rules calculation ko seconds mein kaat dete hain — CSAT ki time-pressure mein yeh game-changer hai.
Concept
  • ÷2: last digit even. ÷3: digit-sum 3 ka multiple. ÷4: last 2 digits 4 se divisible.
  • ÷5: last digit 0 ya 5. ÷6: 2 aur 3 dono se divisible. ÷9: digit-sum 9 ka multiple.
  • ÷11: (odd-place ka sum) − (even-place ka sum) = 0 ya 11 ka multiple.
Dividend = Divisor × Quotient + Remainder
Yahi relation har remainder problem ki jaan hai.

⚡ CSAT Shortcut

Bade power ka remainder nikalna ho toh cyclicity / pattern dhoondo. Jaise 2^n ko 7 se divide karne par remainders 2,4,1 repeat hote hain (cycle of 3).

Solved Example
17 × 23 × 35 ko 5 se divide karne par remainder kya hoga?
(a) 0(b) 1(c) 2(d) 3
Product mein 35 hai jo 5 ka multiple hai → poora product 5 se divisible → remainder 0. Pura multiply karne ki zaroorat nahi.
Answer: (a) 0
Practice — Solve Now
Q1.
Number 3_5 (beech ka digit gayab) 9 se divisible hone ke liye gayab digit kya ho?
(a) 0(b) 1(c) 7(d) 9
Q2.
2^50 ko 7 se divide karne par remainder?
(a) 1(b) 2(c) 4(d) 6
Q3.
Sabse chhota number kaunsa jise 12, 15 aur 20 se divide karne par har baar remainder 5 bache?
(a) 55(b) 60(c) 65(d) 125
AnswersQ1 → b  ·  Q2 → c  ·  Q3 → c

Percentage

Data interpretation, comparison, growth — CSAT ka aadha arithmetic ghoom-phir ke percentage par aata hai.
Concept

Percentage ka matlab "per 100". x% = x/100.

Change % = (New − Old)/Old × 100
Successive change a% then b% → net = a + b + (ab/100) %
  • Fraction ↔ %: 1/2=50%, 1/4=25%, 1/5=20%, 1/8=12.5%, 1/3≈33.33%.
  • Agar A, B se x% zyada hai, toh B, A se x/(100+x)×100 % kam hota hai (ulta seedha nahi).

⚡ CSAT Shortcut

Common fractions yaad rakho. "37.5% of 80" sochne ke bajaye 3/8 × 80 = 30 — instant. Percentage ko fraction mein badalna sabse fast tarika hai.

Solved Example
Ek vastu ki keemat pehle 20% badhi phir 20% ghati. Net kya badlaav hua?
(a) 0% (no change)(b) 4% increase(c) 4% decrease(d) 2% decrease
Net = +20 − 20 + (20×−20/100) = −4 → 4% decrease. Successive change kabhi cancel nahi hota — yahi trap hai.
Answer: (c) 4% decrease
Practice — Solve Now
Q1.
Ek shahar ki aabaadi 25% badhkar 10,000 ho gayi. Pehle kitni thi?
(a) 7500(b) 8000(c) 8500(d) 9000
Q2.
62.5% of 320 = ?
(a) 180(b) 200(c) 220(d) 240
Q3.
A ki salary B se 25% zyada hai. B ki salary A se kitne % kam hai?
(a) 20%(b) 25%(c) 16.66%(d) 33.33%
AnswersQ1 → b  ·  Q2 → b  ·  Q3 → a

Day 4

Average

Average ke sawaal seedhe aur scoring hote hain — agar concept clear ho toh poora marks pakka.
Concept
Average = Sum of observations / Number of observations
Sum = Average × Number  (yeh ulta form hi 80% problems solve karta hai)
  • Consecutive numbers ka average = (first + last)/2.
  • Agar har value mein k jodo/ghatao, toh average bhi k se badh/ghat jaata hai.
  • Weighted average: alag-alag size ke groups ke liye = (n₁a₁ + n₂a₂)/(n₁+n₂).

⚡ CSAT Shortcut

Naya member jud-ne par average ka change = (naye value − purana average)/(naya total count). "Deviation" se socho — har baar pura sum nikalne ki zaroorat nahi.

Solved Example
11 logon ka average umra 28 saal hai. Agar teacher ko milaa lein toh average 1 saal badh jaata hai. Teacher ki umra?
(a) 30(b) 39(c) 40(d) 41
Naya average = 29, total ab 12 log. Teacher = naya sum − purana sum = (12×29) − (11×28) = 348 − 308 = 40.
Answer: (c) 40
Practice — Solve Now
Q1.
5 lagataar (consecutive) numbers ka average 17 hai. Sabse bada number?
(a) 17(b) 18(c) 19(d) 20
Q2.
Ek class mein 20 ladkon ka average 60 aur 30 ladkiyon ka average 70 hai. Poori class ka average?
(a) 64(b) 65(c) 66(d) 67
Q3.
Kisi cricketer ka 10 paari ka average 32 tha. 11vi paari ke baad average 34 ho gaya. 11vi paari mein kitne run banaye?
(a) 52(b) 54(c) 56(d) 58
AnswersQ1 → c  ·  Q2 → c  ·  Q3 → b

Speed, Time & Distance

Trains, boats, relative motion — CSAT ka favourite scoring zone. Ek formula se poora chapter chal jaata hai.
Concept
Speed = Distance / Time
Distance constant → Speed ∝ 1/Time
  • Unit conversion: km/h → m/s: × 5/18  ·  m/s → km/h: × 18/5.
  • Relative speed: same direction → ghatao; opposite direction → jodo.
  • Average speed (equal distance, do speeds) = 2xy/(x+y) — simple average nahi!

⚡ CSAT Shortcut

Train ko pole/man cross karne ka time = train ki length / speed. Platform cross karne mein (train + platform) dono ki length jodo.

Solved Example
Ek aadmi 4 km/h se jaata aur 6 km/h se wapas aata hai. Poore safar ki average speed?
(a) 4.5 km/h(b) 4.8 km/h(c) 5 km/h(d) 5.2 km/h
Equal distance → average = 2xy/(x+y) = (2×4×6)/(4+6) = 48/10 = 4.8 km/h. (5 maan-na hi galti hai.)
Answer: (b) 4.8 km/h
Practice — Solve Now
Q1.
72 km/h ko m/s mein badlo.
(a) 18(b) 20(c) 24(d) 25
Q2.
120 m lambi train 60 km/h se ek khambe ko kitne second mein paar karegi?
(a) 6.2 s(b) 7.2 s(c) 8 s(d) 9 s
Q3.
Do train opposite direction mein 50 aur 70 km/h se chal rahi hain. Relative speed?
(a) 20 km/h(b) 60 km/h(c) 120 km/h(d) 130 km/h
AnswersQ1 → b  ·  Q2 → b  ·  Q3 → c

Syllogism

CSAT ke reasoning section ka pakka topic. Venn diagram seekh liya toh yeh sabse aasaan marks hai.
Concept

Syllogism = di gayi statements ko sach maan kar conclusions check karna. Best tool: Venn diagram.

  • All A are B: A poora B ke andar.
  • Some A are B: A aur B overlap karte hain.
  • No A is B: A aur B alag circles.
  • Rule: Conclusion tabhi sahi jab woh har possible diagram mein sach ho. Ek bhi case fail = conclusion galat.

⚡ CSAT Shortcut

"Some A are B" ka matlab hamesha "Some B are A" bhi hota hai (reverse valid). Par "All A are B" ka reverse "All B are A" zaroori nahi.

Solved Example
Statements: All pens are books. All books are red.  Conclusions: (I) All pens are red. (II) Some red are pens.
(a) Only I follows(b) Only II follows(c) Both follow(d) Neither follows
Pens ⊂ Books ⊂ Red → saare pens red hain (I sahi). Agar pens red hain toh kuch red zaroor pens hue (II sahi). Dono follow karte hain.
Answer: (c) Both follow
Practice — Solve Now
Q1.
Statements: All cats are dogs. Some dogs are cows. Conclusion: Some cats are cows.
(a) Follows(b) Does not follow(c) Can't determine(d) Data insufficient
Q2.
Statements: No man is a stone. All stones are rocks. Conclusion: Some rocks are not men.
(a) Follows(b) Does not follow(c) Both follow(d) None
Q3.
"Some teachers are doctors" sach hai. Toh inme se kaunsa zaroor sach hai?
(a) Some doctors are teachers(b) All teachers are doctors(c) No teacher is a doctor(d) All doctors are teachers
AnswersQ1 → b  ·  Q2 → a  ·  Q3 → a

Day 7

Direction & Distance

Ek rough diagram banao aur sawaal khud solve ho jaata hai — CSAT mein guaranteed easy marks.
Concept
  • 4 main directions: North (upar), South (neeche), East (dayein), West (bayein).
  • Right turn = clockwise (N→E→S→W); Left turn = anticlockwise (N→W→S→E).
  • Shortest distance jab path mein 90° turn ho → Pythagoras: √(a² + b²).
Shortest distance = √(horizontal² + vertical²)
Net horizontal aur net vertical alag-alag jodo, phir Pythagoras.

⚡ CSAT Shortcut

Hamesha North ko upar maan kar paper par diagram banao. Sunrise = East, sunset = West — shadow problems mein direction yahi se pakdo.

Solved Example
Ravi 3 km North chalta hai, phir 4 km East. Starting point se uski seedhi doori?
(a) 5 km(b) 7 km(c) 1 km(d) 12 km
90° turn → Pythagoras: √(3² + 4²) = √(9+16) = √25 = 5 km. Yeh classic 3-4-5 triangle hai.
Answer: (a) 5 km
Practice — Solve Now
Q1.
Ek aadmi East ki taraf munh karke khada hai. Vo right turn leta hai, phir right turn. Ab kis direction mein hai?
(a) North(b) South(c) East(d) West
Q2.
Sita 6 km South, phir 8 km West chalti hai. Starting point se doori?
(a) 10 km(b) 12 km(c) 14 km(d) 2 km
Q3.
Subah ke samay aapki parchhai (shadow) aapke dayein (right) taraf padti hai. Toh aapka munh kis direction mein hai?
(a) North(b) South(c) East(d) West
AnswersQ1 → d  ·  Q2 → a  ·  Q3 → a
